Section Deduction Amount Description
Section 80C 150000 Investments and expenses such as Public Provident Fund (PPF), National Savings Certificate (NSC), Equity-Linked Saving Scheme (ELSS), Sukanya Samriddhi Account, Tuition Fees, etc. are eligible for tax deductions under this section. The maximum limit for tax deductions under this section is Rs 1.5 lakhs.
Section 80CCC 150000 Contributions made to certain pension funds are eligible for tax deductions under this section. The maximum limit for tax deductions under this section is Rs 1.5 lakhs. This section is clubbed with Section 80C, therefore the combined limit of deductions under Section 80C and Section 80CCC is Rs 1.5 lakhs.
Section 80CCD 150000 Contributions made to the National Pension System (NPS) are eligible for tax deductions under this section. The maximum limit for tax deductions under this section is Rs 1.5 lakhs. This section is separate from Section 80C, therefore an additional Rs 50,000 is allowed as deduction under Section 80CCD (1B) for investment made in NPS.
Section 80D 25000 Payments made for health insurance premium for self, spouse and dependent children are eligible for tax deductions under this section. The maximum limit for tax deductions under this section is Rs 25,000 for self, spouse and children. Additional Rs 25,000 is allowed as deduction for senior citizens.
Section 80DD 75000 Expenses incurred for the maintenance of a disabled dependent are eligible for tax deductions under this section. The maximum limit for tax deductions under this section is Rs 75,000 for severe disability and Rs 50,000 for normal disability.
Section 80DDB 40000 Expenses incurred for medical treatment of specified diseases are eligible for tax deductions under this section. The maximum limit for tax deductions under this section is Rs 40,000 for senior citizens and Rs 1 Lakh for very senior citizens.
Section 80E 200000 Interest paid on education loans are eligible for tax deductions under this section. The maximum limit for tax deductions under this section is Rs 2 Lakhs. The tax deduction is allowed for a maximum of 8 years or until the interest is fully repaid, whichever is earlier.
Section 80EE 150000 Interest paid on home loans are eligible for tax deductions under this section. The maximum limit for tax deductions under this section is Rs 1.5 Lakhs. The tax deduction is allowed for the first time home buyers who have taken a home loan of up to Rs 35 Lakhs and the value of the property should not exceed Rs 50 Lakhs.
Section 80GG 10% of (Gross Total Income - HRA Received) Rent paid if the individual does not receive any HRA from the employer is eligible for tax deductions under this section. The maximum limit for tax deductions under this section is 10% of the (Gross Total Income - HRA Received).
Section 80G 50% or 100% of the donation amount Donations made to certain charitable institutions and funds are eligible for tax deductions under this section. The donations made to certain institutions and funds are allowed as deduction under this section. The donations can be claimed as 100% or 50% of the donation amount based on the institution or fund and certain conditions.
Section 80GGC 100% of the donation amount Donations made to political parties are eligible for tax deductions under this section. The maximum limit for tax deductions under this section is 100% of the donation amount.
Section 80TTA 10000 Interest earned on savings bank account is eligible for tax deductions under this section. The maximum limit for tax deductions under this section is Rs 10,000 per financial year.
Section 80TTB 50000 Interest earned on deposit account for senior citizens is eligible for tax deductions under this section. The maximum limit for tax deductions under this section is Rs 50,000 per financial year.
